LinkedIn Classmates

LinkedIn Design Prompt

Duration: ~ 20 hours

Design Prompt

LinkedIn is a place where people can connect to people they know, in order to share professional experience and opportunities. Professional networking can begin at any point, even in school. Increasingly, students are relying on their mobile address book, and not their email address book, to manage their contacts.

Design an experience that helps college students discover and connect to their classmates, without relying on email.


Solution Overview

A mobile app that helps student discover their classmates and engage in discussions to stay connected.


Defining & Research

Initial Brainstorm

With the design problem at hand, I noted down all the available information to define the scope of the project and outlining next steps. Using the whiteboard, I brainstormed on questions and assumptions that I would need to consider to move ahead.


Fig: First whiteboarding

My key questions were:
How do students currently find about their classmates?
How and why do they connect with their classmates?
How do students leverage these connections for job search?



Competitor Analysis

I then analyzed current online networking services that students use to engage and connect with their peers. I learnt that:
Most engaging platforms allow generating social and universal content.
Use of latest technologies like Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning can make conversations more insightful.
Mobile-first approach helps design for users needs.

Fig: Competitor analysis of Facebook messenger, Google plus, Slack and College portal softwares



User Research

I turned to my end users to gather insights on current situation and understand their priorities and pain points. I floated a survey that received 24 responses and then I conducted in-depth interviews with 4 users. The survey results are as below:



Key Findings from user research were:

No dedicated channel to connect with classmates.
“..if I hear about a classmate, I’ll probably look up on Google or LinkedIn and then send a message on Facebook and send files over email. It gets difficult to keep things at one place.”

Poor motivation level to maintain connection with classmates
“I'm really bad at keeping contact with classmates. If they're not in my course group, then I don't really think about them”

No idea on how to use LinkedIn connections
“I don't see a reason to be active on platforms such as LinkedIn as there is not much to do besides add people, update your profile, and search when you are looking for something/someone.”


Persona & User Story

Based on background research and user interviews, I created one primary persona that would help me empathize and design for their pain points and priorities.


I then etched out key user stories for the primary persona to establish the core functionality of the product. This exercise also helped to outline key user tasks.

Ideation & Designing

Design Goals

I took inspiration from Reid Hoffman’s article, “3 secrets of highly successful graduates” and IDEO’s HMW technique to figure out How Might We:
Encourage college students to engage in and share professional discussions at one place,
Motivate college students to learn and follow their peers and create value of networking, and
Use latest technologies to help students make best use of data available to them.
Referring to LinkedIn’s UX design principles, I defined my design goals for this project as:



Approachable

Easy to use and adopt for students

Valuable

Personalize actions to connect with opportunities

Engaging

Facilitate conversation in a fun & delightful way

Sketching and Ideation


After establishing my design goals and user stories, I pulled up my sharpies and paper to sketch basic flows and structure of the system. This helped me through initial ideation phase and refine my flows. Also considered technologies like artificial intelligence, machine learning, data mining, sentiment analysis and crowdsourcing to see how can I leverage those while designing my solution.

Key Design Decisions

1. Use latest and accepted UI patterns of LinkedIn ecosystem.
2. Use better chunking and indexing - using dynamic tags for discussion.
3. Create actionable items through intelligence - view career paths and recommended jobs.
4. Evangelize personalization and customization - sync calendar, contacts and use bookmarks.


Reflections

1. What more? - If given more time, I would include interactions outside of the app (like sync, migration to sister apps), design experience for different entry points like phonebook, emails, etc., and also leverage crowdsourced students data to provide richer insights.

2. User Testing Plan - I would collaborate with UX researchers to conduct A/B testing to implement better UI patterns. I would also conduct usability evaluation sessions to improve insights page so that it makes better sense to users with different needs. If given a chance, meeting with Product Managers would help me define MVP better and come up with simpler solution.

3. Future explorations:
Study smart technologies like AI, AR, Voice or Gesture to make the experience fun and intelligent.
Discover more about student and professional networking methods that could be incorporated.
Study other ways of making the platform more engaging and social yet professional like events, etc.
