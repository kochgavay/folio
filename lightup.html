LightUp

Never Forget to Turn Off the Lights Now!



 Type: Individual Academic Project
Duration: Sept - Nov 2015
Keywords: Energy Efficiency, Mobile Design, Internet of Things, Visual Design

OVerview

Problem

Students and professors at University admit forgetting to switch of lights or fans frequently while rushing for the classes. This habit has made them pay for more and more each month. At a broader level, this practice is continuously undermining the efforts of global energy savings. The goal was to improve userâ€™s electricity usage habits.


Solution Overview

A mobile app to notify users of appliances left turned on when they leave. Following the notification, the user can switch off the appliance and monitor the daily savings. The application would also provide tips on energy savings and comparative performance statistics as against the neighbourhood. Also, sharing such information would enable spreading general awareness among youths to imbibe better efficiency practices in their daily lives.


Role

Being an individual capstone project of a graduate level interaction design course, my role included working on all stages of design from problem defining, ideation, user study, prototyping and analysis.


Process



Define

Conducted an initial study with 5 users to understand their problems. Developed 2 primary, 1 secondary and 1 negative persona to analyse different user goals and needs and also study their behaviour towards the problem. While these personas were developed over the time with several user studies, they also helped me streamline the issue.






Ideation

Brainstormed and sketched 40 scenarios to narrow down the scope of the system. Also, communicating the scenarios to the potential users helped me understand their expecations and refine my design goals.





Design Goals

Based on user feedback and system analysis, I defined my goals to make the interface more functional, user-centric and informative.



Functional

Make sure application serve its purpose and make sense to people.

User-Centric

Design most-intuitive interface with better user experience

Informative

Be able to share information and spread awareness regarding better energy usage

Paper Prototype

In order to achieve the above goals, I made paper prototypes to determine user flow and frame out the information architecture. I used these prototypes to carry out several usability tests that brought out insights on information flow and scope for improving the user experience.





Final Design

Prototype

After the refinements and tests, I created high fidelity prototypes to make sure the interactions and transitions are smooth. Also, it made easier to analyse the user experience by testing on potential users. Click here to try it out.





Takeaways

Empathy

Was able to create interface that would cater to the expectations and frustrations of users

Usability

Carried out Heuristic Evaluation and Usability tests to ensure better user experience.

Rapid Prototyping

Created quick paper prototypes to test with users and refine information flow.
