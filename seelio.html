Seelio

Contextual Inquiry for Seelio



 Type: Academic Group Project (4)
Duration: Jan - Apr 2016
Keywords: Contextual Inquiry, Affinity Wall, User Interview

Overview

Client

Seelio by Keypath is an Ann Arbor based tech startup that allows its users to create an online portfolio of their work and provides a platform to showcase this work to prospective employers. Their affiliations with several departments within universities allows students to put up their work on their Seelio profile as a part of their coursework.



Problem

Lack of user engagement and poor user retention. After completion of course, student drop out of Seelio and opt other platforms for making their portfolios.


Solution Overview

Based on findings from our research and interviews, we recommended solutions in two key areas:
1. Marketing & Strategy Related
a) Improve personalization and customization of data through by allowing universal content and merging with other platforms.
b) Hold student portfolio competitions to increase awareness and user participation and award winners with Job Interviews with sponsors.
2. UI Related
a) Improve information architecture and discoverability of key features.
b) Provide UI controls for layout, color, theme-based templates.


Role

As UX consultants, our role was to identify reasons behind lack of engagement through UX research methods and recommend ideas to promote Seelio among students.

Key Responsibilities: Conduct background research, user interviews, contextual inquiries, prepare affinity wall and present recommendations to client.


Process

Background Research

Studied Seelio’s market presence and functioning over the years. Other team members researched on Seelio’s evolution, strategy and need for e-portfolios. Key finding: For profit purposes, Seelio underwent several strategic changes and modified its mission statement all along. This crippled their user base and ended up with high dropout rates.



User Interview

Interviewed 2 active users, 1 dormant user, Seelio’s design team and its marketing team to know
User’s background and motivation behind building a portfolio,
The pain points faced by the user while going through this process, and
Their experience of using Seelio.

Each of these interview notes were later annotated with insights, user quotes, questions and design ideas.



Affinity Wall

Using the annotated notes, we created the affinity wall and iterated over over it to categorize and re-organize issues and insights. The aim of this exercise was to structure the data collected and uncover meaningful insights or issues through them. Broad categories (green notes) of the findings were:
Seelio’s value and strategy
User engagement with Seelio
Issues with networking and collaboration
Later, we conducted a walk-through of the affinity wall among our peers to get their feedback and refine our recommendations.





Design Recommendations

Based on data from background research, user interview and affinity wall, we drew key findings for which we recommended solutions to improve their user engagement.

1) User engagement is low due to lack of relevant content. We recommended Seelio to allow personalization and customization to make it more relevant.



2) Poor information architecture cripples discoverability of key features. Should work on restructuring and reorganizing data to be more intuitive



3) Project description is too long and discourages further reading. Should provide blurb of projects enough to create curiosity.


Takeaways

Collaboration

Worked as a team to evaluate client's problem and suggest changes

Research Methods

Conducted interviews and affinity notes to qualitatively analyze the issue

Clientele

Worked closely with company's stakeholders to solve their problem.
