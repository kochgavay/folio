Voice for Salesforce

Speech Recognition Experience for Salespeople
(under NDA regulations)



 Type: Product Design
Duration: May 2016 - Aug 2016
Keyword: Speech Recognition, IoT, Alexa Skill, User Research

Overview

Background

Salespeople use Sales Cloud to manage their customers by adding, following up and converting their leads. Efficient use of the app requires salespeople to log all their activities and customer data on Salesforce manually.


Problem Space

Sales representatives collect heaps of notes and artefacts after meetings and find it painful to manually update all of it on Salesforce. Customer data is spread across platforms/ devices and it takes a long time to log it in to the system. This not only affects their performance but also their activity visibility to their managers.


Solution Overview

A voice UI for salespeople to log and retrieve data from Salesforce using voice input, on-the-go. The goal is to ease data logging process by making it real smooth, fast and instant. This not only adds more selling time but also enhaces data visibility to the managers.


Role

As a Product Design Intern, my role was to explore use-cases for voice recognition and design experience for those use-cases using UX methods. My responsibilities also included user research and usability testing.


Process



Domain Study

Studied and analyzed over 50 pages of relevant papers and articles to understand evolution of voice UI and its relevance in user experience. Did 3 competitor analysis to compare features and abilities of key competitors. The key findings from the research were to make the experience simple, intuitive, frictionless and hands-free.


User Research

Conducted user interviews with 7 sales representatives and managers to understand their Salesforce usage pattern, key user flows and pain points. Also, interviewed 2 VPs, 5 PMs and 8 UX designers, engineers & researchers to know about frequent user-flows and use-cases for voice integration.

Key Findigs:

Data Overload

Users end up with heaps of notes at the end of day.

Time Consuming

It takes lot of time to upload data both on desktop and mobile app.

Multiple Devices

Customer data is scattered across multiple devices used by salespeople

Design Goals


Apart from making the experience fast, friendly and fun, I used Salesforce’s design principles, to make sure the voice UI is clear to the users, efficiently performs the tasks, is consistent with the existing experience and is simply delightful to talk to.



Persona, Scenario & Voice Interactions

In collaboration with the user research team, identified the role-based primary persona as the Deal Closer or sales rep and secondary persona as the Ring Leader or sales manager. Based on the use-cases and pain points from the research, I created a day-long story of a sales rep performing key user flows using voice. Used voice interaction map to design “direct dialgoues” that could later be scaled for “natural language”.




Design & Prototype

Designed voice experience for salespeople to update information into Salesforce. Also, leveraged IoT to auto-update information based on location, time and customer activity.

Collaborated with UX engineering team to develop Alexa skills for use-cases and integrate with Salesforce API. Later, tested the prototype with users using guerilla testing method. Details of the prototype are hidden as per the NDA regulations.





Future Recommendations

Due to time constraint of 12 weeks, I wasn't able to conduct further testing and research. Some of the recommendations for future work include:
Explore opportunity for creating voice personality.
Design grammars & user training
Building for value, trust and intelligence

Takeaways

Empathy

Understood user pain points and designed for frictionless experience.

Enterprise Product

Learnt key flows, user needs and constraints of enterprise solution product.

Non-traditional UX

Explored and learnt how voice UI is different from traditional UX
