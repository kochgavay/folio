Museum of Interactive Arts

An Interactive Approach to Public Space Design



 Type: Bachelor's Design Thesis
Time: August - December 2011
~ Design Research, Feasibility Studies, Physical Prototype, Space Design ~

Overview

Problem Statement

With mass development of interactive digital media, there has been a shift of the masses to the virtual world. This phenomenon has led to 'social withdrawal syndrome', where people shut off any interaction with the outer physical world that ultimately contributes to the deterioration of public exhibition spaces.


Solution

Architecture and space design has been shaping lives and culture since ages and its high time to merge it with the growing immersive technology and digital media. Including interactive installations in public spaces like museums could be a way to draw the digital culture back to physical built environment and also prove to be a great learning platform.


Role

Bachelor's Design thesis was an individual project mentored by a professor and stretched over a period of six months. My role included conducting a research on interventions of digital media in physical spaces, study related works and propose a design scheme. I presented the final proposal to the city's renowned team of architects in the final jury.



Process

Research

Conducted literature and case study of 9 digital installations in public spaces to analyze the following:
Types of human activities in Public Spaces and their movement pattern.
Modes of user engagement in such spaces. (Gesture, touch, motion and speech)
Categorization of spaces based on required level of interaction. (Entrance hall, Art Gallery, Plaza)
User reaction and behavior while interacting with the interface.
Level of awareness and education through digital media.




Fig: Examples of digital installations with user engagement in public spaces.





Findings

Using the results of the study, I established 5 major attributes of digital installations as participative, practical action, desired context, new experience, and transformative. This helped me to frame the design goals and work on incorporating necessary features into the digital spaces.




Fig: Classification of related projects based on user experience criteria


Design Goals

Engage

Spaces should engage people with artefacts and themselves and inspire them to visit regularly.

Educate

To promote learning and education about the artefacts through better digital experience

Excite

The heirarchy of spaces and arrangement of artefacts should create a storyline to encourage exploration




Proposal

Design Proposal

Several iterations helped lay out the spaces based on user movement, artefact arrangement, open spaces, climatology, site conditions and navigational approach. Strategically placed building blocks close to each other create an open social space in between that would lead to the plaza on the north side. This concept encourages more social engagement and avenues for digital interaction.





Prototype

Created physical and digital prototypes for the building design to evaluate the proportions and space quality.
