ATLAS

A digital tangible display to increase awareness about the student's activity



 Type: Tangible Interaction Design
Time: October 2015 (Ongoing)
Keywords: IoT, Product Design, Data Visualization

Overview

Project Goal

To increase awareness of teaching and learning activities within a department among instructors to build a sense of community and increase discussion of pedagogy. More than half of instructors at University of Michigan agreed to the fact that they had no idea what the students are doing in the class or if they are interested at all.


Solution Overview

A tangible digital art display that visualizes studentâ€™s activities in class or off-class throughout the semester. Determining in-class attentiveness through gaze-tracking and observing course involvment via university portal would keep instructor aware of the overall class engagement.


Role

Product Designer. Key Responsibilities: Lead a 3 member and design the art display, background research, brainstorming, material preparation, assembly, installation and user behavior study.



Research & Design

Research

Studied over 25 related research papers and 10 relevant projects on ambient awareness to analyse the following:
What is the type of User Experience?
Is the visual/audio signal abstract or direct?
Is the signal helping in decision making?
What is the user's level of interaction?
Is there a direct or indirect mental mapping?

Key Findings:
1) Digital art displays should have visualization that prompt direct action like assignment completion.
2) Product should allow physical engagement to have deeper knowledge of the subject like information table or timeline slider.



Design Process

We integrated raspberry pi with University portal to fetch activity of students from 17 departments at UofM. We then connected LEDs to visualize retrieved live data in a Voronoi pattern. These LEDs are plugged into plexiglass fixed in birch plywood to be installed as a wall display in library/ faculty lobby. The art display reveals current status of student activity and suggests scope for better engagement.





This project is currently at a preparation stage and will be installed in the University library/ faculty lobby to facilitate usability and behavioral studies. The project is also on-road for paper and poster presentation at the Learning Analytics Conference 2017.
