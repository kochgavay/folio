RateWait

Google Design Prompt

Duration: ~ 20 hours

Design Prompt

While there are many ways to rate and review restaurants, these are not focused on evaluating individuals servers. Design an experience where diners can submit positive comments and constructive suggestions for the wait staff, and servers can use this feedback to both improve and help to secure new employment. Provide a high-level flow and supporting wire frames.


Solution Overview

A cross-platform application that helps waitstaff to use diner's feedback to improve and secure better jobs.


Defining & Research

Initial Brainstorm

With the design problem at hand, I noted down all the available information to define the scope of the project and outlining next steps. Using the whiteboard, I brainstormed on questions and assumptions that I would need to consider to move ahead.


Fig: First whiteboarding

My key questions were to A) understand how diners currently provide feedback to waitstaff, B) how does a waitstaff look for new jobs and what is their learning process, and C) how do restaurant manager hire waitstaff and what qualities do they look for.


User Research

I turned to my end users to gather insights on current situation and understand their priorities and pain points. I interviewed 3 servers, 2 diners and 2 restaurant managers to find out:
1. Current job search method is through Craigslist ads, walk-in interview and mostly through word of mouth or referrals.
2. Current feedback method is through restaurant websites, paper slips, and online surveys. Server reviews do exist but its rare.
3. Diners look for punctuality, preparedness, attentiveness, knowledge of menu, manners and presentation.

Key pain points were:
1. No dedicated channel to rate servers.
2. Waitstaff cannot keep record of guest feedback to put to constructive use.
3. Too many resources for wait staff education but no personalized improvement plan.

Ideation

Persona & User Story

Based on background research and user interviews, I created two primary persona and one secondary persona that would help me empathize and design for their pain points and priorities.

Primary Persona: Waitstaff/ Server and Diner/ Guest
Secondary Persona: Restaurant Hiring Manager

I etched out key user stories for the primary persona to establish the core functionality of the product. This exercise also helped to outline key user tasks.

Design Goals

I used HMW Technique to figure out How Might We:
a) encourage guests to leave feedback for servers,
b) makes sense of guest feedback in the best possible way, and
c) make an engaging learning platform for servers. Based on that, I established my key design goals as:

Functional

Enable key tasks completion with good user experience

Cross-Platform

Allow reviewing from multiple devices

Engaging

So that servers can use it as a powerful self-development tool

Sketching and Wire-framing


After I established my design goals and user stories, I pulled up my sharpies and paper to sketch basic flows and structure of the system. This helped me through initial ideation phase and refine my flows. I also considered latest technologies like artificial intelligence, machine learning, data mining, sentiment analysis and crowdsourcing and explored how can I leverage those while designing my solution.

Design Decisions

1. Use quickest and smooth review collection method - gathering most relevant info in shortest time.
2. Display guest feedback data in an insightful way that triggers an action for waitstaff.
3. Maintain visual consistency across devices and experiences.
4. Levarage information from user interviews like providing job recommendations to users.


Reflections

1. What more? - I would love to explore how waitstaff can add new skills recommended by guests to keep their profiles updated. I would also work on how incentivize or gamify the education experience for servers to increase user-engagement.
2. User Testing Plan - I would conduct various usability evaluations methods to refine the home/ insights page of server side. I would also conduct A/B testing of diner experience to figure out faster and more user friendly way to collect reviews.
3. Future explorations:
Integration with existing reviewing platforms like Yelp, Google, Urbanspoon, etc
Adding social aspect - making it collaborative learning space
Other ways of user input like gesture, voice and tangible interactions.
Connecting waitstaff to educational institutes for better learning.
